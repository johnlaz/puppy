Tips for the Drive Home with Puppy

🌟 The Most Important Thing

Everything will seem overwhelming for the puppy at first. That's totally normal! A calm, quiet voice and gentle handling goes a long way. You've got this.

  • 🚗
    Keep the puppy in the crate in the car. It's the safest place and helps them feel secure. Put an old t-shirt with a familiar smell in there for comfort.
  • ⏱️
    Stop every 2–3 hours for a potty break. Take the pup to a grassy area on leash. Young puppies have small bladders — don't wait too long.
  • 🥣
    Don't feed a big meal right before driving. A light snack is fine. Puppies can get car sick. Ask the breeder about their last feeding time.
  • 💧
    Offer water at every stop, not in the moving car. Too much water motion can upset a young tummy. Short drinks at rest stops are perfect.
  • 🎵
    Keep the car calm and quiet. Soft music is fine. Avoid loud radio or excitement. A sleepy puppy is a happy road-trip puppy.
  • 🌡️
    Never leave the puppy alone in a parked car. Even with windows cracked, cars heat up dangerously fast. If you stop to eat, take turns so someone always stays with the pup.
  • 📸
    Take tons of pictures! First moments in the car, first time looking out the window, first rest stop. These are memories you'll treasure forever.

At the Breeder — What to Do & Ask

This is the big moment! Take a breath, take it slow, and go through this checklist before you leave the breeder. It is easy to forget things when you are excited.

📷 First Things First

Take lots of photos and videos the moment you meet the puppy — with the breeder, in their home environment, first reactions. You will cherish these forever.

📋 Documents to Collect Before You Leave
  • ✓
    Vaccination records — written proof of all shots given so far (distemper, parvo, etc.)
  • ✓
    Health certificate — signed by the breeder’s vet, confirms the puppy was healthy at departure
  • ✓
    AKC or registration papers — if the puppy is registered, get the paperwork now
  • ✓
    Breeder’s phone number written down — not just saved in a phone, written on paper too
  • ✓
    Microchip number — if the puppy has been microchipped, get the chip ID number
💬 Questions to Ask the Breeder
  • ?
    “What food is the puppy eating?” — get the exact brand and type. Switching food suddenly can upset a puppy’s stomach.
  • ?
    “How many times a day does the puppy eat, and how much?” — write this down.
  • ?
    “When did the puppy last eat and drink?” — helpful to know before the drive home.
  • ?
    “Is the puppy on any medications or dewormers?” — get the name, dosage, and schedule.
  • ?
    “What vet have you been using?” — useful context if questions come up later.
  • ?
    “Any quirks or things we should know?” — breeders know their puppies best. Let them share.
📦 Ask for a Small “Comfort Kit”
  • ✓
    A small unwashed cloth or towel from the puppy’s sleeping area — the familiar smell helps enormously in the car and first nights at home.
  • ✓
    A small amount of the puppy’s current food to mix with new food during the transition at home.

Emergency Contacts & Key Info

🏥 If the Puppy Seems Unwell on the Road

  • Pull over safely. Stay calm — puppies pick up on your anxiety.
  • Check: is it just car sickness? (drooling, whimpering, vomiting) — this is very common and not dangerous.
  • Give fresh air, offer small sips of water, and rest for 20–30 minutes.
  • If symptoms are severe (prolonged vomiting, lethargy, trouble breathing) — call an emergency vet.
